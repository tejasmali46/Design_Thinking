What's Happening to Our River?

The Indrayani River faces several pollution challenges that threaten its ecosystem and our community's health.

Main Causes

  • Untreated sewage from growing population
  • Industrial runoff from nearby factories
  • Plastic waste and ritual offerings
  • Agricultural runoff with pesticides

Environmental Effects

  • Reduced oxygen levels killing fish
  • Algal blooms from excess nutrients
  • Destruction of aquatic ecosystems
  • Contamination of groundwater

Health Risks

  • Increased waterborne diseases
  • Skin infections from contact
  • Contaminated crops when used for irrigation
  • Respiratory issues from water foam

How We Can Help

Everyone has a role to play in saving the Indrayani River. Here are practical solutions for different groups.

1For Pilgrims

  • Use clay or natural idols that dissolve
  • Avoid plastic bags and packaging
  • Use flowers without synthetic wrapping
  • Participate in eco-friendly rituals

2For Locals

  • Properly dispose of household waste
  • Participate in regular river cleanups
  • Build simple trash traps for neighborhood drains
  • Report pollution incidents promptly

3For Authorities

  • Install sewage treatment facilities
  • Enforce regulations on industrial discharge
  • Implement waste management systems
  • Create buffer zones along riverbanks

Why the Indrayani Matters

Understanding the historical and spiritual significance of our river.

Spiritual Heritage

The Indrayani River has been sacred to Alandi for centuries. It is intricately linked with Sant Dnyaneshwar, whose legacy continues to draw pilgrims from across India. The river is not just a water body but a living connection to our spiritual traditions.

Our Mission

We are committed to restoring and protecting the Indrayani River for future generations. Our work focuses on education, community action, and advocacy with local authorities to ensure sustainable river management.

Join us in this sacred duty to preserve Alandi's lifeline and heritage.
